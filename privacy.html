Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2025 · Version 1.0
Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains how Covenant Crest Group Ltd collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who We Are (Data Controller)

The data controller responsible for your personal information is:

Covenant Crest Group Ltd
Registered in England and Wales
Company Registration Number: 16528951
Telford, Shropshire, United Kingdom
Email: info@covenantcrest.co.uk

If you have any questions about how we handle your data, please contact us at the address above with the subject line “Data Privacy Enquiry”.

2. What Personal Data We Collect

We collect personal data through the forms on our website. The specific data collected depends on which form you complete:

General Contact Form:

  • Full name, email address, telephone number
  • Enquiry type and message content

Haulage Quote Request:

  • Name, company name, email address, telephone number
  • Collection and delivery locations, load type, weight and vehicle preferences
  • Preferred service level and additional requirements

Trade & Import Enquiry:

  • Name, company name, email address, telephone number
  • Product requirements, order quantities, frequency, preferred trade terms (Incoterms)
  • Additional specification or requirements

We do not collect sensitive personal data (such as health information, financial account details, or biometric data) through our website forms.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data only for the purposes for which it was submitted:

  • Responding to your enquiry and providing the information or services requested
  • Processing haulage and trade quotations
  • Communicating with you about your enquiry or ongoing business relationship
  • Maintaining internal records of business interactions
  • Complying with our legal and regulatory obligations

We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling, nor do we use it for unsolicited marketing communications.

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we rely on the following lawful bases:

  • Contract performance — processing is necessary to respond to your request for a service or quotation (Article 6(1)(b)).
  • Legitimate interests — responding to enquiries and maintaining business records (Article 6(1)(f)), where our interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Consent — where you have explicitly given consent, such as for the use of non-essential cookies (Article 6(1)(a)). You may withdraw this at any time.

5. Cookies

Our website uses cookies. We categorise these as follows:

  • Essential cookies — necessary for the website to function correctly (e.g., storing your cookie consent preference). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies — used to understand how visitors interact with the site, allowing us to improve it. These are only set with your consent.

You can manage your cookie preferences via the banner shown on your first visit, or by clearing your browser’s cookies at any time.

6. How We Share Your Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties. We may share your data only with:

  • Netlify, Inc. — our website hosting and form-processing provider. Form submission data is processed via Netlify’s servers. Netlify is based in the United States (see Section 8 on international transfers).
  • Our staff and authorised personnel — who need access to respond to your enquiry.
  • Legal and regulatory authorities — where we are required by law to disclose information.

Any third parties we use as data processors are bound by appropriate data processing agreements.

7. How Long We Keep Your Data

Data TypeRetention Period
General enquiriesUp to 12 months from last contact, then deleted
Haulage & trade enquiriesUp to 12 months, or 7 years if a contract arises (legal/accounting requirement)
Business transaction recordsUp to 7 years (HMRC and Companies Act obligations)
Cookie consent preferencesUntil you clear your browser storage

After these periods, data is securely deleted or anonymised.

8. International Transfers

Netlify, Inc. is headquartered in the United States. When you submit a form on our website, your data is transferred to and processed in the USA. Netlify ensures appropriate safeguards are in place for such transfers in accordance with UK GDPR requirements (including Standard Contractual Clauses). For full details, please review Netlify’s Privacy Policy.

9. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

RightWhat It Means
Right of accessRequest a copy of all personal data we hold about you (a “Subject Access Request”).
Right to rectificationRequest correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
Right to erasureRequest deletion of your personal data, subject to any legal obligations to retain it.
Right to restrictionRequest that we limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
Right to portabilityRequest your data in a structured, machine-readable format for transfer to another controller.
Right to objectObject to processing based on legitimate interests; we will then stop unless we have compelling grounds.
Right to withdraw consentWithdraw any consent you have given at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please email info@covenantcrest.co.uk with the subject line “Data Rights Request”. We will respond within one calendar month, and free of charge for most requests.

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. Our website is served over HTTPS (TLS encryption). Form data is processed via Netlify’s secure, SOC 2-certified platform. Admin access to collected data is protected by password authentication.

No transmission over the internet can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. If you have concerns about the security of your data, please contact us immediately.

11. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, please contact us first at info@covenantcrest.co.uk and we will endeavour to resolve your concern.

If you remain unsatisfied, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK’s supervisory authority:

Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO)
Website: ico.org.uk
Helpline: 0303 123 1113

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of the page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our website after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
